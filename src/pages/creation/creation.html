Building the Land, Climates, and Winds

Creating Animals and Man
    Scarcely had He separated all these within certain limits, when the stars, which had lay hidden under that mass, began to blaze out in the whole sky. An animal more sacred than these, and more capable of a lofty mind, and might be able to rule of the rest, was still wanting. The Golden Age
    The Golden Age was born first, which, with no avengers, cultivated faith and right by its own free will without law. Aurea prima sata est aetas, quae vindice nullo, sponte sua, sine lege fidem rectumque colebat. Punishment and fear were absent, nor were threatening words fixed on brass, poena metusque aberant, nec verba minantia fixo aere legebantur, nor the begging throngs feared the look of judges, and they were safe without vigilantes. nec supplex turba timebat iudicis ora sui, sed erant sine vindice tuti. Pines felled were not yet descended down their mountains into the liquid waves, nondum caesa suis, peregrinum ut viseret orbem, montibus in liquidas pinus descenderat undas, and mortals knew no shores except their own. nullaque mortales praeter sua litora norant; Nor did rain-filled moats surround the towns. nondum praecipites cingebant oppida fossae; There were no trumpets of straight, not horns bended brass, non tuba derecti, non aeris cornua flexi, nor helmets nor swords: the nations secure spent soft leisure without the use of soldiers. non galeae, non ensis erat: sine militis usu mollia securae peragebant otia gentes. Terra, Herself also free and untouched by the harrow, nor wounded by any ploughshares, gave all things of itself. ipsa quoque inmunis rastroque intacta nec ullis saucia vomeribus per se dabat omnia tellus, All were living in peace as Terra gave her fruits without cultivation or labor. Being content, Terra created food without constraint, culled fruit and mountainous strawberries and cornels and blackberries sticking on harsh brambles and acorns which had fallen from the spreading tree of Jupiter. Terra erat contenta cibis nullo cogente creatis arbuteos fetus montanaque fraga legebant cornaque et in duris haerentia mora rubetis et quae deciderant patula Iovis arbore glandes. There was an everlasting spring, and gentle zephyrs fanned with warm breezes the flowers born without seeds. ver erat aeternum, placidique tepentibus auris mulcebant zephyri natos sine semine flores; Soon also the Earth unploughed bore fruits and with no renovation the fields whitened with heavy ears of corn. mox etiam fruges tellus inarata ferebat, nec renovatus ager gravidis canebat aristis; Now rivers of milk, now rivers of nectar were running, and yellow honies distilled from the verdant holm oak. flumina iam lactis, iam flumina nectaris ibant, flavaque de viridi stillabant ilice mella.
