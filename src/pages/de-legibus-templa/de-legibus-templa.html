De Legibus TemplaThe Laws of the Temples

Definitions

Ab aeternoFrom the Enternal
Gods and their cults that came from before earliest times of the universe.
Ab antiquoFrom Antiquity
Cults recognized as observing beliefs predating the Religio Romana Roman Religion.
Ab extraFrom Without
Cults that do not follow in the Roman tradition.
Ab initioFrom the Cradle
Cults established by King Numa Pompilius.
Cessante ratione legis cessat ipsa lex
When the reason for the law ceases, the law itself ceases.
Consuetudo pro lege servatur
Custom is held as law.
Creatio ex nihilo
Creation out of nothing
Delegata potestas non potest delegari
Delegated powers can not be further delegated
Deus ex machina
A god from a machine
Do ut desI give that you may give
Ductus exemplo
Leadership by example
Durante bene placitoDuring good pleasure
Serving at the pleasure of the authority or officer who appointed
Entia non sunt multiplicanda praeter necessitatemEntities must not be multiplied beyond necessity
Ex officioFrom the office
When someone holds one position by virtue of holding another.
Ex oriente luxLight from the east
Flamen
A high priest dedicated to the service of a particular God or Goddess
Pontiff
A high priest
Sacerdos
A priest
Sacerdos domusPriest of the house
The head of the household (regardless of sex/gender) who leads the sacred rituals and rites of the house in honor of Vesta, the household Gods and Goddesses, and the family genius.
Scriptorwriter, author, scribe
An official writer or contributer of the Libris Sanctis
